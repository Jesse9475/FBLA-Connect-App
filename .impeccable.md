## Design Context

### Users
FBLA Connect is used by Future Business Leaders of America members — high school and
college students who compete in business, leadership, and entrepreneurship events.
Context: used in classroom, competition prep, and between events. Users are ambitious,
achievement-oriented, and technically comfortable. Advisors also use the app to manage
chapters and communicate with members.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: Sharp. Earned. Forward.**

The app should feel like something a student is proud to show during a presentation —
polished, purposeful, not generic. It should carry the weight of competition and
achievement without feeling cold or corporate. Every interaction should feel decisive.

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Contender" — Editorial precision meets competitive achievement.**

A complete departure from the previous industrial navy/glassmorphism look.
New foundations:
- **Typography**: Josefin Sans (display/titles — geometric, architectural, unmistakable)
  + Mulish (body — warm, readable, humanist). JetBrains Mono only for actual data values.
- **Colors**: Electric blue (#2563EB) as the interactive/brand color — vivid, energetic,
  clearly visible against dark surfaces. FBLA gold (#F5A623) reserved strictly for
  achievement moments: CTAs, competition badges, featured highlights.
- **Dark mode**: True editorial dark (#09090E near-black, not navy). No blue-tinted surfaces.
- **Light mode**: Warm off-white (#F8F7F3 — paper-like) with #0D0C17 ink text.
- **System-adaptive**: Both modes should feel equally designed.
- **No glassmorphism** on cards (reserved only for the floating nav bar).
- **No left-accent border strips** — typography and spacing carry hierarchy.
- **No double-bezel construction** — single clean surface with hairline border.

Anti-references: the previous dark navy + glassmorphism + JetBrains-Mono-everywhere look;
generic "educational app" with bright primary color everywhere.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography leads** — Josefin Sans at the right weight creates more impact than any
   decorative element. Let the type do the visual work.
2. **Gold is earned** — Use gold only for CTAs, achievements, and featured moments.
   Electric blue handles all interactive/link states. Never use both in the same element.
3. **Space is a material** — Generous padding and clear section breaks communicate quality.
   Tight, cramped UIs feel cheap regardless of their colors.
4. **Data uses mono, everything else doesn't** — JetBrains Mono is reserved for
   timestamps, stat numbers, codes, and member IDs only. Not for labels, not for buttons.
5. **Dark is dark, light is light** — Dark mode is #09090E, not navy. Light mode is
   warm paper, not blue-tinted white. Each mode is fully designed, not just color-inverted.
